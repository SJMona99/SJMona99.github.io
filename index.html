Research

Responsive Route examines how Emotion AI and material intelligence can transform architecture into a living interface—spaces that sense, interpret, and adapt to human behavior and feeling.

Methods include biometric sensing (heart rate, EDA), parametric systems, and real-time control to prototype emotionally aware environments.

About

I am a Computational and Parametric Designer exploring the intersection of architecture, artificial intelligence, and human emotion. My current research, Responsive Route, investigates how Emotion AI and material intelligence can transform architecture into a living interface—spaces that sense, interpret, and adapt to human behavior and feeling.

With dual master's degrees in Architecture and Advanced Computational Design, my work bridges design logic, data-driven systems, and aesthetic expression. I use parametric and generative design methods to prototype environments that respond to biometric and environmental data, creating meaningful emotional connections between people and space.

By combining computational workflows with affective computing and material performance, I aim to push the boundaries of responsive architecture—crafting spaces that not only function, but feel.
