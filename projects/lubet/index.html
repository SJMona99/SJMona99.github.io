The Lubet — Interactive Kinetic Installation

Lubet is an interactive kinetic installation that translates human motion into mechanical movement using Arduino-controlled servos. This work explores light, reflection, and motion through a tessellated sculpture driven by responsive control systems.

Overview

The Lubet sculpture responds to body movement and presence, turning human gesture into mechanical actuation. Its faceted geometry catches and multiplies light, creating a constantly changing field of reflections. The project combines digital design, physical prototyping, and embedded electronics to create a spatial, performative object.

The installation was developed through iterative modeling, fabrication tests, and on-site calibration of servo motion, timing, and light conditions.
